# TrustLens Detection Signals

Signals are **heuristic indicators**, not proofs.

Each signal answers one question:
> “Does this content resemble known misinformation patterns?”

---

## Core Signal Categories

### 1. Sensational Language
Detects:
- Emotional manipulation
- Clickbait phrasing
- Excessive urgency

Examples:
- “Doctors hate this”
- “Before it’s deleted”
- “Shocking truth”

---

### 2. Absolute Claims
Detects:
- Guarantees
- Certainty language
- Miracle promises

Examples:
- “Cures all diseases”
- “100% guaranteed returns”

---

### 3. Forwarded Message Patterns
Detects:
- Viral copy structures
- Chain-message language
- Anonymous sourcing

---

### 4. Lack of Evidence
Detects:
- Claims without sources
- Vague authorities
- Unverifiable statements

---

### 5. Fake Headline Structures
Detects:
- Misleading summaries
- Out-of-context framing

---

### 6. Testimonials & Reviews
Detects:
- Anecdotal persuasion
- Emotional testimonials
- No verifiable identities

---

### 7. Political Messaging
Detects:
- Manipulative framing
- Fear-based narratives
- Oversimplified blame

---

### 8. Financial Promises
Detects:
- Guaranteed profits
- Risk-free investment claims

---

## Signal Philosophy

Signals are:
- Additive, not absolute
- Context-aware
- Continuously evaluated

No single signal determines the result.
